## P0.5 — related-work correction (exact prose to insert)

> Two concurrent audits of subject identity in EEG foundation models are complementary to ours but ask
> different questions. **Lin, Wu, and Jung (arXiv:2606.06647)** study *marginal* subject dominance in frozen
> foundation-model features — LEACE-based erasure, the spectral carrier of subject identity, and the
> consistency of the task axis — i.e. a geometry/erasure audit of the marginal subject signal.
> **Tai (arXiv:2606.09189)** is a *privacy / attribute-leakage* study auditing cross-encoder attribute
> transfer (whether attributes leak across independently trained encoders), not a marginal subject-variance
> audit. We therefore do **not** describe both as "marginal subject-variance audits." Crucially, FMScope's
> cohort-conditioned *frozen-feature* diagnostic (an oracle that fits the erasure geometry on the whole
> cohort, including the held-out subject's features) and our strict source-only *unseen-subject deployment*
> ask different questions and **can both be correct**: cohort-conditioned geometry can be erasable while the
> same axis is not identifiable, or removable, from source subjects alone (P0.5 bridge, oracle vs source-only).

## P1.5 — terminology & notation rules (apply throughout)

1. "Barber–Agakov-style plug-in" → **"cross-fitted neural posterior plug-in estimate"** (no bound is proved).
2. Separate symbols: `L_CMI_train` (in-loop training regularizer) vs `Î_audit` (held-out audit estimate).
3. State explicitly that finite-critic underfitting can UNDER-report leakage (already present in §3/§7;
   reinforce with the P1.3 multi-capacity familywise-max underfitting guard).
4. Keep raw nats; add normalized `Î / Ĥ(D|Y)` as a secondary cross-dataset readout only.
5. Never write "exact CMI", "unbiased", "universal upper bound", "calibrated bits" (grep-gate; CIGL LaTeX
   already avoids these — keep it that way).
6. Do NOT claim subject erasure GENERALLY fails: utility is representation-, protocol-, and
   task-structure-dependent.
7. Do NOT claim TOS improves DG: TOS is a geometry diagnostic / guarded intervention with an identity
   fallback.
8. Decoder-side CMI stays OUT of the core contribution and experiments this revision.
9. `claim_evidence_table.md` maps every quantitative claim to a script/artifact/aggregation/seeds/folds/CI +
   allowed/forbidden wording.
