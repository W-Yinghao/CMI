\section{Route inclusion ledger}
\label{app:routes}

The audit spans 37 routes across the repository's measurement/control lines. Under the Phase-1
quantitative-inclusion gate (Section~\ref{sec:ladder}), a route enters a cross-level test only if it
carries $\geq 1$ predictor-side level \{L1--L4\} \emph{and} $\geq 1$ endpoint-side level \{L5,L6\}. Six
routes qualify; the remainder are support/boundary/protocol/background and inform interpretation only.
No target-label-fit route enters any quantitative test.

The excluded routes fall into four tags: \textsf{SUPPORT\_ONLY} (a predictor or endpoint level is
present but not the pairing a specific RQ needs --- e.g.\ FBCSP branch load is L4-only), \textsf{BOUNDARY\_ONLY}
(a boundary/decision result such as the erasure refusal gate), \textsf{PROTOCOL\_ONLY} (an executed
protocol without an efficacy endpoint), and \textsf{BACKGROUND\_ONLY} (context lines, e.g.\ the
prior-decoupled adaptation decomposition). The full per-route ladder mapping, missing-metric policy,
and target-label tags are released with the artifacts.
