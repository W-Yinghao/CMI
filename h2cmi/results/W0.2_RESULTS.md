# W0.2 — Fixed-reservoir prevalence intervention (corrected q-grid)

**Headline: FRSC attenuates prevalence leakage but is not prevalence-invariant; displacement is not the
same as utility.**

- **Status:** COMPLETE · 90 (pair,subject) units (72 (dataset,subject)); q-grid {0.1..0.9}, anchor 0.5
- **Design:** one fixed reservoir per unit; the same trials reweighted (effective-count weights) at each q
  (trial identity + temporal position invariant). Source seeds {0,1,2} averaged within unit; cluster
  bootstrap by (dataset, subject), 10k.
- **Data:** `w02_displacement.csv`, `w02_utility.csv`, `wave0_v2p.report.json`
- **Provenance:** reuse of frozen V2P bundles (`v2_bundles`/`p0_v2pw_bundles`); branch
  `exp/h2cmi-wave0-mechanism`. (Non-deterministic aggregate curves, like the terminal V2P.)

    pooled ≈ 0.08  <  FRSC ≈ 0.42  <  fixed_iterative / joint ≈ 0.80  ≪  oracle ≈ 2.0

| operator | embed-disp q0.1 | q0.9 |

FRSC moves clearly more than the prevalence-agnostic `pooled` baseline → **fixed-reference soft
conditioning attenuates prevalence leakage but does not remove it**; it is strongly attenuated vs the
label oracle. `fixed_iterative`/`joint` move ~2× more than FRSC.

## Displacement ≠ utility

Under balanced accuracy, prevalence-induced movement is largely **invisible in BA**: FRSC BA is flat across
q ([0.520, 0.524, 0.519] at q=[0.1,0.5,0.9]). So latent-geometry movement (displacement) can occur without
BA utility. The utility consequence depends on the **metric** — see W0.5.

| FRSC | q0.1 | q0.5 | q0.9 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| BA | 0.520 | 0.524 | 0.519 |
| ordAcc(unif) | 0.708 | 0.524 | 0.515 |
| ordAcc(oracle-q) | 0.731 | 0.524 | 0.548 |

## Reading

Fixed-reference soft conditioning attenuates but does not eliminate prevalence sensitivity; and the
displacement it produces is **not** the same thing as decision utility. Whether the prevalence movement
is harmful, benign, or useful depends on the evaluation metric — quantified in `W0.5_RESULTS.md`.
