## Primary question

Does increasing adaptation batch size remove the W2 decision-prior harm?

## Primary conclusion

**No. Increasing n makes `P_J` more negative. Small adaptation batches partially mask the balanced-accuracy
metric-prior mismatch.**

## Decomposition by n (exact: `P_J = metric-mismatch + transfer/noise + π_J-deviation`)

| n | P_J | metric-mismatch | transfer/noise | π_J deviation | H(π_J) | min π_J | TV(π_J,ρ_A_full) | missing |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 16 | −0.092 | −0.162 | −0.010 | **+0.080** | **1.442** | 0.085 | 0.253 | 0.92 |
| 32 | −0.110 | −0.162 | −0.006 | +0.058 | 1.367 | 0.060 | 0.251 | 0.53 |
| 64 | −0.124 | −0.162 | −0.005 | +0.043 | 1.292 | 0.038 | 0.261 | 0.34 |
| 128 | −0.133 | −0.162 | −0.006 | +0.035 | 1.234 | 0.023 | 0.263 | 0.23 |

(`transfer/noise` = `B_E(ρ_A^draw) − B_E(ρ_E)`, the draw-prevalence / sample-noise term — small and
roughly constant. Rows sum to `P_J` exactly; residual 5.6e-17.)

## Endpoint contrasts (n=256 minus n=16, paired subject-cluster)

- **Δ P_J = −0.0445 [−0.054, −0.036], significant.**
- **Δ π_J-deviation effect = −0.0500 [−0.061, −0.040], significant.**
- Δ metric-mismatch = 0.0, invariant.
- Δ TV(π_J, ρ_A_full) = **+0.018 [+0.001, +0.034], barely significant and *opposite* to pointwise
  convergence.**

## Interpretation (TV caveat explicit)

The frozen directional prediction is confirmed for the decision-prior branch: **larger adaptation batches
worsen `P_J`.** The mechanism is the shrinkage of the positive `π_J`-deviation offset (from +0.080 to
+0.030) against a **constant** negative metric-mismatch anchor (−0.162).

This should **not** be described as pointwise convergence of `π_J` to the full adaptation prevalence,
because `TV(π_J, ρ_A_full)` is flat or slightly increasing. The supported mechanism is **decision-effect
convergence / prior sharpening**: as adaptation batch size increases, `π_J` becomes **less uniform** and
its **BA decision effect moves toward the oracle-prevalence penalty** — entropy decreases (1.442 → 1.188)
and the minimum fitted mass decreases (0.085 → 0.014) — even though pointwise TV to the full adaptation
prevalence is flat or slightly increasing. Larger batches **de-regularize / sharpen** the fitted prior;
they do not make `π_J` converge (in TV) to `ρ_A`.

## What it closes

The reviewer objection *"is the W2 prior harm just a small-adaptation-batch artifact?"* is decisively
answered: **no — small batches partially mask the harm; larger batches make it worse.** The small-batch
`π_J` is accidentally more uniform (regularized), which is *less* harmful for balanced accuracy; as the
prior sharpens with n, the harm is exposed. Even a sharper, better-identified prevalence-like prior is the
wrong balanced-accuracy decision prior.
