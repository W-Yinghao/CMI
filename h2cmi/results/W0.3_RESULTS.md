## Primary mechanistic conclusion

The W2 decision-prior harm is dominated by **balanced-accuracy metric-prior mismatch**. Using the **oracle**
evaluation prevalence `ρ_E` as the decision prior already produces the full negative `P` effect. The
adapt/eval prevalence-transfer term is negligible, and `π_J`'s deviation from `ρ_A` **partially offsets**
the oracle-prevalence harm rather than causing it.

`ρ_E` is an **oracle diagnostic** (it uses evaluation labels only to localize the mechanism); it is **not**
a deployable unlabeled procedure.

## Mechanistic decomposition

`B_E(π)` = balanced accuracy of the identity-geometry decoder `argmax_y[log p(z|y)+log π_y]` on the
evaluation set `E`. Exact identity: `B_E(π_J)−B_E(Unif) = [B_E(ρ_E)−B_E(Unif)] + [B_E(ρ_A)−B_E(ρ_E)] +
[B_E(π_J)−B_E(ρ_A)]`.

| term | cross-night | same-session |
|---|---:|---:|
| `P_J = B_E(π_J) − B_E(Unif)` | −0.144 [−0.160, −0.128] | −0.134 [−0.150, −0.118] |
| BA metric-prior mismatch: `B_E(ρ_E) − B_E(Unif)` | **−0.162 [−0.181, −0.144]** | **−0.161 [−0.179, −0.143]** |
| adapt/eval prevalence transfer: `B_E(ρ_A) − B_E(ρ_E)` | −0.0055 [−0.016, +0.005] **NS** | 0.0000 exact |
| `π_J` deviation effect: `B_E(π_J) − B_E(ρ_A)` | +0.024 [+0.007, +0.041] | +0.028 [+0.012, +0.043] |

The metric-mismatch term is **protocol-invariant** (−0.162 vs −0.161): the harm is intrinsic to using
prevalence as a decision prior under a balanced metric, not to cross-night shift or π_J quality.

## Per-stage recall (identity geometry, same-session)

| decision prior | W | N1 | N2 | N3 | REM |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Unif | 0.92 | 0.29 | 0.88 | 0.52 | 0.63 |
| `ρ_E` (oracle) | 0.94 | 0.055 | 0.95 | 0.14 | 0.30 |
| `π_J` | 0.95 | 0.002 | 0.80 | 0.49 | 0.32 |

The oracle prevalence prior improves majority-stage recall but collapses minority and boundary-stage recall
under a balanced metric: N1 0.29 → 0.055, N3 0.52 → 0.14, REM 0.63 → 0.30, while N2 rises 0.88 → 0.95. Thus
the core failure is **not** poor prevalence estimation — even the exact evaluation prevalence is the wrong
decision prior for balanced accuracy. (`π_J` then further crushes N1 → 0.002 but recovers N3 → 0.49, which
is why its net deviation term is positive; see `w03_stage_decomposition.csv`.)

## Approved one-paragraph statement

> Sleep-stage prevalence is a poor balanced-accuracy decision prior even when known exactly and even when
> adaptation and evaluation prevalence are matched. The dominant W2 failure is metric-prior mismatch, not
> cross-night transfer or poor π_J estimation. In aggregate, π_J's deviation from the adaptation prevalence
> partially offsets the oracle-prevalence harm rather than causing it.
