# W0.5 — Metric-dependence of the decision prior

**Headline: decision priors are metric-dependent, but prevalence-aware decisions are only useful when the
model supports them. The metric switch is real but specification-dependent.**

- **Status:** COMPLETE (analyzer-only over W0.2 + prior-decomp logs; no extra model runs)
- **Data:** `w05_v2p_switch.csv`, `w05_sleep_lens.csv`, `wave0_w05.report.json`
- **Design:** two lenses. (i) V2P controlled 2-class prevalence intervention (oracle-q decision prior),
  cluster bootstrap by (dataset,subject), 10k. (ii) Sleep natural ordinary accuracy `Σ_y ρ_E[y]·Recall_y(π)`
  under decision priors {Unif, ρ_A, ρ_E, π_J}, subject cluster.

## The switch, per lens

| lens | ordinary-accuracy switch? | value |
|---|---|---|
| **V2P / FRSC, q=0.1** | **yes (significant)** | ordAcc(oracle-q)−ordAcc(unif) = **+0.0225 [+0.014, +0.032]** |
| **V2P / FRSC, q=0.9** | **yes (significant)** | **+0.0333 [+0.022, +0.046]** |
| V2P / FRSC, q=0.5 | no (balanced → null) | ≈ 0 |
| V2P / joint | no (NS) | ≈ 0 — geometry movement dominates/disrupts decision-prior utility |
| **Sleep, natural ordinary accuracy** | **no** | uniform dominates **both** BA and ordinary accuracy |

For FRSC, using the oracle-q decision prior **significantly improves ordinary accuracy at both prevalence
extremes**, while the same prevalence-aware rule is **harmful for balanced accuracy** (BA is flat, favoring
uniform). That is the metric rule: BA uses a uniform decision prior; ordinary accuracy may use a
prevalence-weighted prior when the deployment prevalence is the target objective and the score model
supports that decision rule.

However, the switch is **specification-dependent**. In natural sleep staging, prevalence-aware decoding does
**not** improve ordinary accuracy — uniform decoding dominates both metrics (BA(Unif)=0.66 vs ρ_E 0.49;
OrdAcc(Unif)=0.77 vs ρ_E 0.73). This is consistent with source/adapter misspecification or calibration
failure; we do not claim a proven causal diagnosis. The defensible statement: **prevalence-aware decision
rules are not automatically decision-useful under real source/adapter misspecification.**

## Operational rule (the paper's clean position)

> Use a uniform decision prior for balanced accuracy. Use prevalence-aware decisions only when the
> deployment objective is prevalence-weighted **and** the score model supports that decision rule. Estimated
> or oracle prevalence is not automatically decision-useful when the metric changes.

This defeats both strawmen:
- **"Estimated prevalence is always bad."** No — it significantly helps ordinary accuracy in controlled
  V2P/FRSC.
- **"Use estimated prevalence whenever prevalence shifts."** No — it hurts BA (W0.3), and in natural sleep
  it does not even help ordinary accuracy.

## Pairing with W0.3

- **W0.3:** even oracle sleep-stage prevalence is the wrong decision prior for **balanced accuracy**
  (metric-prior mismatch dominant, −0.16).
- **W0.5:** prevalence-aware decisions **can** help **ordinary accuracy** in a controlled, well-specified
  prevalence intervention (V2P/FRSC, significant), but **not automatically** under natural sleep
  misspecification.

Together they give the cleanest operational rule in the paper: the correct decision prior is
**metric-dependent and specification-dependent**.
