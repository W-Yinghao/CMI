\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Concept-shift certification is, at bottom, an \emph{information} problem. An unlabeled target marginal cannot
settle a claim about the conditional $P(Y\mid Z)$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:impossible}), so the honest output of
a deployment-time certificate is not a binary shift/no-shift label but a three-state decision with an explicit
abstention branch.

Under a single freeze-before-confirmatory discipline, a source-anchored $Z$-only certificate failed its frozen
confirmatory on unseen synthetic clusters, whereas a paired within-subject, minimal-label certificate ---
supplying targeted target-side information absent from the $Z$-only setting --- passed its frozen, unseen,
independently red-teamed confirmatory within a pre-registered synthetic envelope. The contribution is thus a
falsifiable certification framework and an information ladder from abstention to confirmation, together with the
audit protocol that produced both verdicts --- not a universal detector. All results are synthetic; real-EEG
certification remains future work.
